Hydrogen Atom Orbital

This program calculates the wavefunction of the non relativistic hydrogen atom, solving the Schr√∂dinger equation using separation of variables between the radial part and the spherical harmonics (angular). It takes as imputs the Principal (n), Azimuthal (l) and Magnetic (m) quanum numbers. The numbers must be integers and follow these rules:
n = 1, 2, ...
l = 0, 1, ..., n - 1
m = -l, ..., 0, ..., l
The output is a cool looking 3d plot, showing the eigenstate of the calculated hydrogen atom.


Generalization of the Fibonacci Sequence

Who doesn't know the Fibonacci sequence? If you're a coder, studied some math related science or even, had a good teacher at school, you may be familiarized with this sequence. Well, this program goes one step beyond (or maybe more than one simple step), because it generalize the sequence to the whole complex plane (as inputs and outputs), and also gives you a cool visual representation of "what's going on here"... To be honest, this is one of those things that make me love math, not just because of the beauty behind the complex-valued algebra but also for the challenge of translating them to a computer program.
